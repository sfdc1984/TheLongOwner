Hidden Honda Decisions

Why I Stopped Guessing My Car's Width

Two raised lines on the bonnet — and why they matter more on a ghat road than in a showroom.

Observation

I noticed it on a hairpin, not in a brochure.

Climbing a ghat section with a rock face on one side and a drop on the other, I caught myself glancing at the front corners of the bonnet instead of my mirrors to judge how much room I had. I hadn't consciously decided to do this. It had just become the way I placed the car.

Back in flatter city driving, the same habit showed up squeezing through narrow lanes and between parked cars. Long before I knew there was a name for it, I was using the shape of the bonnet itself as a reference point.

Engineering Explained

What are bonnet flanks?

Bonnet flanks are the raised character lines running along the shoulders of the hood, sitting just inside your peripheral view over the dashboard. Because they stay visible from the driver's seat at almost every angle, they give a constant, low-effort visual cue for where the car's left and right edges actually are — without needing to glance down at a mirror or guess.

In my ownership experience, this matters most exactly when attention is already stretched thin: a narrow ghat bend, a gap between two trucks, reversing into a tight spot. The line doesn't demand attention. It's simply there, in view, doing the job quietly.

DriveLab Insight
You don't calculate the width of your car. You feel it — and the bonnet flanks are quietly teaching you where the edges are.
Try It Yourself

Next time you're on a narrow lane or a hairpin bend, notice where the bonnet's shoulder line sits relative to the road's edge before you check your mirror. Then check the mirror anyway — you'll likely find your instinct was closer than you expected.

Evidence
  • Based on repeated ghat-road and hairpin driving
  • Consistent across narrow city lanes and parking manoeuvres
  • Aligned with general research on peripheral visual reference cues
  • Continuously reassessed as more routes are driven
